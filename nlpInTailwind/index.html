Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is an AI branch that aims to bridge the gap between computers and human language. Its primary goal is to enable computers to comprehend, interpret, and produce human language in a meaningful and contextually relevant manner. This interdisciplinary field integrates computer science, linguistics, and cognitive psychology, propelling advancements in virtual assistants, chatbots, sentiment analysis, language translation, and much more. NLP's continuous growth and integration into various industries highlight its profound impact on transforming how humans interact with machines in today's technology-driven world.

Tokenization

The process of breaking down a text into smaller units, such as words or sentences, called tokens. Tokenization is a crucial step in NLP, as it prepares the text for further analysis.

Text Preprocessing:

Involves tasks like removing punctuation, converting text to lowercase, and handling stop words (commonly occurring words like "the," "is," etc.) to clean and normalize the text data.

Learn More

Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging:

Assigning a grammatical category (noun, verb, adjective, etc.) to each word in a sentence. POS tagging helps in understanding the syntactic structure of a text.

Learn More

Named Entity Recognition :

Named Entity Recognition (NER) is an NLP technique that identifies and classifies named entities in a text, including people, organizations, locations, and dates. It plays a vital role in various applications, like information extraction and language understanding.

Learn More

Sentiment Analysis:

Sentiment analysis, a vital NLP task, gauges emotions in text—positive, negative, or neutral. It's extensively applied in social media monitoring and customer feedback analysis to comprehend public opinion and sentiment trends.

Learn More

Text Classification:

Text classification, an essential NLP technique, sorts text into predefined categories like spam detection, topic classification, and sentiment analysis. This enables efficient information organization and enhances decision-making processes.
